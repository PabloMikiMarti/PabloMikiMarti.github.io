About

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at Brigham and Women's Hospital and Harvard Medical School, with an affiliation to the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. My research focuses on the intersection of machine learning and healthcare, specifically in improving clinical trial efficiency and providing decision support tools for cardiologists.

I completed my PhD at Universitat Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona, where I developed novel machine learning methodologies for managing heterogeneous clinical data. My work has been supported by the Fundacio Privada Daniel Bravo Andreu, and I have collaborated with leading experts in cardiology and machine learning.

Passionate about data-driven personalized medicine, I aim to contribute to advancements in cardiac care through innovative research and interdisciplinary collaboration.
